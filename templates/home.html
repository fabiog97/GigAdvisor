Overview

In the cloud-based society, where ICT mediates most of the social, economic, and personal interactions, technology is no longer merely a subject of regulation but is becoming an integral part of the regulatory process.
  Techno-regulation, the “intentional influencing of individuals’ behavior by building norms into technological devices,” (Leenes 2013), is inspiring new ways to support legal safeguards through hardware and software tools, technical solutions allowing to create legally relevant relations, hamper breaches of law and promote norm compliance.
  Stemming from a research in the area of computational legal studies, Gig Advisor is an experimental web application dealing with the techno-regulation paradigm and its potential application in the world of Digital Labor Platforms.
  The goal of the initiative is twofold. On the one hand, it aims at exploring regulatory effects that can be induced, in the digital platform market, by tools exploiting reputational mechanisms. On the other hand, it aims to gather data to be used in computational social science research on the gig economy. The the tool, still under development, currently encompasses three basic components:

  • A module allowing crowd workers to evaluate digital labour platforms they work for, according to a series of predefined criteria (e.g. employer fairness, payment timeliness).
  • A module for the real-time analysis and visualization of data resulting from the assessments made by crowd workers.
  • A forum allowing crowd workers to discuss issues and share ideas about their working experience.
